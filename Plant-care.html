Guide to Greener Living

Making Plant Care Easy, Engaging, and Accessible.

Live Website
September, 2023

Tags: UX/UI Design Wireframing & Prototyping HTML, CSS, JavaScript User Research & Testings Information Architecture Accessibility (WCAG) Responsive Web Desig

Challenges

    • Information overload – Users struggle to find simple, reliable, and structured plant care advice.
      •  
    • Poor navigation & UX – Many plant care sites lack a clear structure, making it difficult to find answers.
      •  
    • Lack of personalization – Most sites treat every user the same, but beginners need different guidance than experienced plant owners.

My Impact

70% of testers reported that they could find plant care information within 10 seconds.

Users rated navigation 4.5/5 in post-testing surveys.

Bounce rate reduced by 30% during usability testing (users stayed longer).

01. User Research: What Users Needed

Before jumping into design, I conducted user research to understand real pain points.

| User Surveys & Interviews |

  • 15 users surveyed (Google Forms)
  • 5 in-depth interviews (Zoom & in-person)
  • Participants: Plant owners, office workers, wellness practitioners

key takeaways

Both personas prioritize access to reliable information, but their specific needs vary: Sarah needs efficient, office-oriented guidance, while Alex seeks practical, engaging plant care knowledge.

Time constraints and the desire for ease of use are important factors for both.

Trustworthiness and clarity of information are crucial for meeting user needs.

| Information Architecture & UX Flow |

I sketched a sitemap that ensures logical content flow, reducing cognitive load and enhancing usability. Every feature is structured to minimize frustration and provide quick, intuitive access to key information

The Quiz: A Future Enhancement One of my initial goals was to include an interactive quiz to personalize plant care recommendations. However, due to project time constraints, I faced a choice: force an incomplete feature or prioritize a seamless core experience. I chose clarity over complexity.

02. Defining the Problem

Plant owners need an easy-to-navigate, visually engaging, and informative platform to help them confidently care for their plants.

03. Ideation

Plant owners need an easy-to-navigate, visually engaging, and informative platform to help them confidently care for their plants.

| How Might I ? |

  • ...make plant care more intuitive and engaging?
  • ...structure information so users can find answers quickly?
  • ...personalize the experience based on user skill level?

| Key Solutions Explored |

  1. Step-by-step plant care guides with intuitive navigation.
  2. A quiz-based recommendation system to tailor content to users’ experience levels.
  3. A mobile-first, responsive design for seamless access anywhere.

04. Design & Prototyping

| Method for creating contents |

  • Concise Language - Used simple, clear language to break down complex topics, ensuring users aren’t overwhelmed by too much detail.
  • Actionable Advice - Focused on providing practical, real-world tips that users can immediately apply to plant care.
  • Clear Structure - Organized the content into distinct, purposeful sections (e.g., soil types, propagation) for easy navigation and a logical flow
